The capital of France is Paris. Paris is known as the City of Light and is famous for its iconic Eiffel Tower, world-class museums like the Louvre, and charming cafés along the Seine River.

France is a country in Western Europe. It shares borders with several countries including Germany, Belgium, Luxembourg, Switzerland, Italy, Spain, and Monaco. The country has a rich history dating back to ancient times.

Paris has been the capital of France since 987 AD when Hugh Capet, Count of Paris, became King of France. Before that, the capital was located in other cities such as Aachen and Orléans.

The French Revolution began in 1789 and led to significant political and social changes in France. The revolution ended the monarchy and established the First French Republic.

Today, France is a major economic and cultural power in Europe and the world. Paris remains one of the most visited cities globally, attracting millions of tourists each year who come to experience its art, cuisine, and romantic atmosphere.
